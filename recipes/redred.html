Ghanaian Red-red

Ghanaian Red Red Stew is a simple, heartwarming dish from Ghana. It’s vegan, gluten-free, and… it’s pretty darn delicious. Made with black-eyed peas in a rich tomato base, this Ghanaian red red stew is a good addition to your healthy lunch or dinner menu. Here is a recommended list of steps to make it.

Here is everything you will need to make this delicious vegan stew:

  • black-eyed peas
  • Palm Oil
  • Onions
  • Bell Peppers
  • Tomatoes
  • Bouillon cubes
  • Tomato sauce

Now that you've sorted the ingredients out let's get to making some Fried Plantain and Beans

  1. First of all, you have to soak the black-eyed peas. You can do that by washing the dried beans and then soaking them for about 8 hours overnight.
  2. Once they are soaked long enough, drain the black-eyed peas and wash them again with water. Place the black-eyed peas in a medium-sized pot and pour enough water to cover them, and the water level is about two inches above the black-eyed peas. Place the pot over medium heat and let the black-eyed peas cook for about 60 minutes or until they are tender. Once done, set them aside.
  3. Heat two tablespoons of palm oil in a large pot. Add chopped onion and cook for about 3 minutes until they are soft.
  4. Add grated ginger, minced garlic, bell pepper, and green onion. Sauté for a minute or two. Next, add chopped tomatoes, tomato sauce, bouillon, cayenne pepper, curry powder, black-eyed peas, and black pepper.
  5. Mix well and let it cook for about 10 minutes. Using a potato masher, mash black-eyed peas slightly so that the stew gets creamy.
  6. Add two tablespoons of palm oil and Season it with salt.Garnish with gari powder if you like. Serve with either baked or fried plantains.
